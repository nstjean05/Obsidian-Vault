# Lecture Notes
## Regimes
- Regime: Form of government and the underlying principles that provide the legitimate basis for that form of government
- Regime = logic
	- Institutions and systems flow from that logic
## Equality
- Equality and liberty
- Only political equality is guaranteed in the constitution
- Representative Democracy - Parliamentary Democracy
	- The crown was kept as a last resort, given the distrust of the American system
## Liberty
- Liberal democracies have two underlying principles
	- We are free to do whatever we with, given that no law prohibits us
- Natural rights vs. Utilitarian logic
- Utilitarianism gives rights based on context
- This is a distinction from the U.S.
## General Rights
- Protection of the private sphere
- Respect for minority rights
- Rule of law
- Constitutionalism - supreme laws, which limit what the government can do
## Consent
- We consent to being under the regime, and will obey its laws
- At confederation, two regimes created:
	- Liberal democracy
	- Colonists (leading to T&R)
