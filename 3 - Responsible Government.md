## 3.1 The Emergence of Responsible Government
- This is "A Constitution Similar in Principle of That of the United Kingdom"
- Mid 1700s, Montesquieu sees that Britain has transformed from a tyrannical monarchy to a prime example of separation of powers
	- Protects freedom and political abuse
	- Builds on Lockean ideals
- The American extended Montesquieu's principles to (in addition to separating legislative/executive) choose their leaders via democratic election
- In early British rule, colonies such as Nova Scotia gained the right to elect
- Upper Canada (Ontario) had difficulty in the early days, as reformers could take control of the legislative branch, but the governors were still controlled by a wealthy elite
	- This led to armed rebellions in 1837, which although crushed by British troops, showed the need for reform
