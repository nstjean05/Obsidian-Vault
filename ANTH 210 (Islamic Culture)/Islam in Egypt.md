## Overview
- North African country, partially on the Sinai peninsula
- Population of ~110 million
- Major trade centre
## Introduction to Islam
- Islam arrived in 7th century AD by the Arab Muslim conquests, led by Amr ibn al-As
- Egypt was taken from the Byzantines
## Branches of Islam
- Majority Sunni
- Al-Azhar is a major Islamic institution
- Small Shia muslim minority
- Some Egyptians practice Sufism
## Islamic Law
- Islam is the state religion
- Sharia law is the main source of law
- Hanafi school is the main school
	- Affects marriage, divorce, and inheritance
- Egypt is not a theocracy
## Holy Sites
- Al-Azhar Mosque
	- Center of Islamic learning, more than 1000 years old
- Al-Hussein Mosque
	- Contains remains of historic figure
- Islamic cultural centre
- Sites used for worship and education
## Islam and the Government
- Egypt is a republic
	- President and parliament
- Islam influences legislation, but doesn't dictate it
- Government and religious institutions work together on Islamic issues
## Recent Events
- Muslim Brotherhood political influence
	- Islamic political group in 2012
	- Removed by military in 2013
- Increased government control of religious groups
