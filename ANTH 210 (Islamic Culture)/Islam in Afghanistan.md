### Modern History
- 1933-1973
	- Zahir Shah
	- Constitutional Monarchy
- 1973
	- King overthrown by his cousin
	- Ends monarchy
- 1978
	- Communist Revolution
	- Called the Czar Revolution
	- Land redistribution, education controls
- 1979-1989
	- Soviet invasion of Afghanistan
- 1989-1996
	- Civil war
- 1995-2001
	- Rise of the Taliban
	- Originated with students who wanted to restore order
	- 1996 the Taliban capture Kabul
	- Strict following of rules
- 2001
	- U.S. invasion of Afghanistan after 9/11
	- Collapse of the Taliban
- 2001-2021
	- 20 year war
	- Slow return of freedoms
- 2021-present
	- U.S. leaves and Taliban rapidly regains control

### Islam
- Shapes their life, law, and national identity
- Significant private/public life split
- Religious scholars interpret legal decisions
- Imams are especially influential rurally
	- Mosques function as schools in rural regions
- Some Afghan leaders believe Islam can exist in a modern world, but this created doubts that the Taliban capitalize on
### Women
- Banned from education
- Can't travel more than 72km without a man
- Must wear a Hijab, enforced by morality police
- Must remain silent in public
- This was not always the case, but has been since the 2021 Taliban takeover
