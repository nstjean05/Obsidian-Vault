### Monasticism as Protest and Reform
1. Concern
	- Monks thought you couldn't pursue a Christian life in Christendom
	- Alludes to the Essenes in the desert, who thought that urban Judaism was corrupt
	- A worldly church
2. Protest
	- Spiritual but no religious - disengage and pursue some kind of non-Christian spirituality
3. Martyrdom and Monasticism
	- Pre-Christendom, martyrdom was a way for elite Christians to demonstrate their eliteness
		- Apex of self-sacrificing detachment
	- When Christendom arises, martyrdom is no longer a possibility, as the state will no longer prosecute you
		- Early monastics would then go out into the desert
		- 'Spiritual Martyrdom'
		- Sacrifice of the body
	- Goal to save your soul, deny the body, and pray for the church
	- Alternative is to go the fringe of the empire and evangelize pagan groups
		- Many of these Christians were also killed
